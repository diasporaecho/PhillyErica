The Itinerary

Seven Days, Block by Block

Grouped by neighborhood so the walking stays short and the story stays whole — from the founding ground to the long memory.

01
Day One
Old City · Independence District

Where Freedom Was Founded

Orient on the ground where the republic — and its contradictions — were set in stone.

  • The President's House Site — the only federally funded memorial to the enslaved, marking where Oney Judge freed herself.
  • Independence Visitor Center — the self-guided Black History Marker map.
  • Welcome dinner — a celebrated Black-owned kitchen.
After Dark Stratus Rooftop, Old City — a skyline DJ set to start the trip right.
02
Day Two
Old City · Arch Street

Audacious Freedom

The founding story of Black America, told by the first museum a major U.S. city ever built to honor it.

  • African American Museum in Philadelphia — opened 1976; the Audacious Freedom exhibit & Jack T. Franklin photo collection.
  • The Black Journey — Old City Tour — James Forten & Oney Judge.
  • Free Quaker Meeting House — the abolitionist network.
After Dark Bok Bar — 360° skyline at golden hour, no velvet rope.
03
Day Three
Society Hill · Graduate Hospital

The Mother Church & the Seventh Ward

The spiritual and intellectual heart of the route.

  • Mother Bethel A.M.E. Church — founded by Richard Allen, 1794; oldest land continuously owned by Black Americans.
  • The Black Journey — Seventh Ward Tour — Du Bois's The Philadelphia Negro.
  • W.E.B. Du Bois marker.
After Dark Kitchen & Kocktails — soul food and over-the-top cocktails on South Broad.
04
Day Four
South Broad · Center City

The Sound of Philadelphia

From the Lincoln Memorial to the producers who built a genre — the day the heritage sings.

  • National Marian Anderson Museum — the contralto barred from Constitution Hall (reserve ahead).
  • Music Alliance Walk of Fame — Coltrane, Patti LaBelle, Gamble & Huff.
  • Live jazz — the Sound of Philadelphia legacy.
After Dark SOUTH Restaurant & Jazz Kitchen — the city's premier Black-owned jazz room, live till late.
05
Day Five
Historic Germantown

Attics of the Underground Railroad

The cobbled avenue where Quaker families hid freedom-seekers.

  • Johnson House Historic Site — Philadelphia's only intact UGRR stop; sheltered Harriet Tubman.
  • The Colored Girls Museum — by appointment.
  • ACES Museum — a restored WWII USO for Black soldiers.
After Dark 8Twelve Lounge — soul food and early-2000s R&B in a grown-and-classy room.
06
Day Six
West Philadelphia

Robeson & the Living Corridor

Among the artists and the everyday culture of West Philly.

  • Paul Robeson House & Museum — the icon's final-decade home, now a cultural center.
  • 52nd Street & Mural Arts — the historic Black main street.
  • Bushfire Theatre — Walk of Fame.
After Dark Sunset Social — golden-hour rooftop and Friday block parties in West Philly.
07
Day Seven
Strawberry Mansion · North Philadelphia

Strawberry Mansion & the Long Memory

From the house where Coltrane reshaped jazz to the burial grounds of the abolitionists — closing the circle.

  • John Coltrane House — where he composed early masterworks (landmark/exterior visit, under restoration).
  • Bethel Burying Ground Memorial — resting place of thousands of early Black Philadelphians.
  • Farewell dinner — a final Black-owned table and a toast to the long memory.
After Dark Assembly Rooftop at The Logan — a parkway-skyline toast to close it out.
Three Lights Beyond · Days 8–10

Carry It Past the City

Add any or all to stretch the route from seven days to ten.

Day 08

The First HBCUs

Cheyney & Lincoln · Day Trip

Cheyney (1837, the oldest HBCU) and Lincoln (1854) — alma mater of Langston Hughes, Thurgood Marshall, and Kwame Nkrumah. Where Black higher education began.

Day 09

The Heritage Coast

Atlantic City & Southern NJ

The African American Heritage Museum of Southern NJ — 12,000+ items, the Club Harlem legacy — and Chicken Bone Beach, the Black beach of the segregation era.

Day 10

Lines to Freedom

Kennett Square · Brandywine

The Kennett Underground Railroad Center — guided tours of safe houses and Quaker meetinghouses on the routes north.
